Jason Nazary Solo

Jazz / Electronic / Experimental

Lineup

Jason Nazary dr

For this concert, the 3G rule applies: Entry is only authorized with a Swiss or EU Covid-Certificate (QR Code) for vaccinated, recovered or tested persons with an official identification (ID, passport, driver’s license). The detailed admission rules are listed here: https://www.moods.club/en/program/admission-rules-covid/

Jason Nazary is a drummer/producer/improviser/composer from Atlanta and based in Brooklyn. Fascinated by the intersection of acoustic and electronic music, Jason has been a force in New York's creative music scene for over a decade. He's released two records under his solo project, So Ghost, that explore the use of acoustic drums as trigger and control sources for electronic instruments in the context of raw, minimalist compostion and improvisation. This summer Jason made his We Jazz Records debut with "Spring Collection", released on 25 June. The album sees Nazary crafting some deliciously sparkly solo cuts plus working long distance with choice collaborators Jaimie Branch, David Leon, Ramon Landolt, Matt Mitchell, Grey McMurray and Michael Coleman. This is essentially a collection of home recordings and the whole operation has an infectious feeling of immediacy to it. The result is improv adjacent electronic music, with modern production aesthetics transposed over spontaneous compositions. He will be performing selections from "Spring Collection" arranged for solo drums & electronics.

Chris Wiesendanger Solo

Jazz

Lineup

Chris Wiesendanger p

In the music of Chris Wiesendanger, concentration and presence are at the center. Polyphonic and polyrhythmic music unfolds from this still center. Multi-layered sound fabrics and superimposition of movement sequences stand in contrast to emptiness and open space. The music spreads out, creates resonance, brings about change, and returns to silence.

His solo concerts are deeply rooted in the jazz piano and blues traditions, but he is also intimately familiar with the literature of classical and new music. He explores traditional playing techniques as well as new ways of creating sound inside the piano. He also works on extensions, overdubs and changes to the acoustic piano sound through electronics.Live stream
